The trouble with Bears

By Evan Wild

Tall, lumbering, angry, dangerous. The real live bears of this world are proud, independent creatures, self-serving and always on the hunt for food. Nothing like the bears you see on TV.

So what are bears really like, and why does the world's media portray them incorrectly?

Types of bear

Bears come in two varieties — large and medium. If you have seen a small bear, it was a cub.

Bears can also be classified by their habitat. The table below gives useful facts.

Bear Type Coat Adult size Habitat Lifespan Diet
Wild Brown or black 1.4 to 2.8 meters Woods and forests 25 to 28 years Fish, meat, plants
Urban North Face 18 to 22 Condos and coffee shops 20 to 32 years Starbucks, sushi

Habitats and Eating habits

Wild bears eat a variety of meat, fish, and fruit. They live in caves, tents, or cottages.

Urban bears prefer scavenging dumpsters, bins, or breaking into kitchens.

Mating rituals

Bears are romantic creatures that prepare attractive living spaces to woo mates.

About the author
